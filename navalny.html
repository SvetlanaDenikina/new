Alexei Navalny: Life as a struggle for the beautiful Russia of the Future

Alexei Navalny (born June 4, 1976 in the Moscow region) is a Russian political and public figure, lawyer and video blogger. The leader of the “Russia of the Future” Political Party, the leader of the Russian opposition. He gained initial fame for his investigations into corruption in Russia. Creator of the "Anti-Corruption Fund", author of YouTube channels: "Alexei Navalny", "Navalny LIVE" and "Popular Politics".

He took second place in the 2013 Moscow majoral election, receiving 27.24% of the vote and losing to Sergei Sobyanin. In December 2016, he announced his intention to participate in the presidential elections of Russia, which took place in March 2018. According to the editorial board of “Vedomosti” newspaper, he was actually the only politician who conducted a full-fledged election campaign in 2017, but on December 25, 2017, the Central Election Commission refused to register him due to an outstanding criminal record in the Kirovles case.

In 2017-2018, Alexei Navalny was the leader of the mass protest movement in Russia.

Projects of the "Anti-Corruption Fund":

  1. RosPil (2012): lawyers of the organization sent complaints about potentially corrupt tenders for 59.1 billion rubles.
  2. RosYama (2011):the service made it possible for motorists throughout Russia to file complaints about bad roads in automatic mode.
  3. RosVybory (2012): observers prepared by RosVybory revealed many violations in the presidential elections.
  4. RosZhKH (2012): the site has helped tens of thousands of users to achieve solutions to utility problems.

Since the beginning of the 2010s, Alexei Navalny has been an accused, defendant and witness in a number of criminal, administrative, civil and arbitration cases that international human rights organizations consider politically motivated. The European Court of Human Rights ruled in favor of Navalny, and the cases were found to be falsified. The ECHR also recognized seven episodes of Navalny's detention and administrative arrests in 2012-2014 as politically motivated.

Recognition:

  • "Person of the Year" according to the "Vedomosti" newspaper (2009);
  • Participant of the "100 most influential people in the World" rating according to TIME magazine (2012, 2021);
  • Member of the list of the "25 most influential people on the Internet" according to TIME magazine (2017);
  • "Politician of the Year" according to the “Vedomosti” newspaper (2017, 2019);
  • 4th place in the rating of trust of Russians according to the research center "Romir";
  • The status of "prisoner of conscience" from the human rights organization Amnesty International;
  • Winner of the Sakharov Prize (awarded in 2021 by the European Parliament);
  • Nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize.

On August 20, 2020, as a result of poisoning with a chemical warfare agent from the Novichok group, Alexei Navalny fell into a coma. From August 20 to 22, he was in the toxicoreanimation department of the Omsk City Clinical Hospital No. 1, and from August 22 to September 22, he was treated at the Charite Hospital in Berlin, after discharge he underwent rehabilitation in Germany. On January 17, 2021, after treatment in Germany, Navalny returned from Berlin to Moscow, where he was arrested during passport control.

On March 22, 2022, the Lefortovsky District Court of Moscow sentenced Alexei Navalny to 9 years of imprisonment in a high-security colony. At the time of the website's creation, Navalny has been in prison for 629 days.
